What is Resilience?

Resilience refers to the ability of individuals and communities to prepare for, respond to, and recover from disasters and crises. Key factors that contribute to resilience include:

  • Access to resources and information.
  • Strong social networks and community support.
  • Robust infrastructure and emergency services.
  • Education and training on disaster preparedness.

Building resilience is essential for minimizing the impact of disasters and ensuring sustainable recovery.
